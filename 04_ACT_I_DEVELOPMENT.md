# Global Act I conditions

## Starting condition

Solomon Oku is dead.

His sons know him primarily as a controlling, withholding father in Vancouver. They do not know the human history of his life in Calabar or the bloodline's responsibility to the family land.

No supernatural force is active around them.

## Ending condition

Kai has:

- left the marked path
- found the balance shrine
- broken the red-thread seal
- removed the talisman
- experienced inherited resonance
- raised the talisman toward restoration, then chosen not to replace it

The regulator is open. The bloodline has been recognized. The first black spider has emerged.

The ashes remain in the single brass urn with Malik, Ade, and Bode above the ravine.

## Act I dramatic question

Will the brothers carry Solomon's last request together?

Answer:

Yes - but their decision returns them to a responsibility Solomon concealed.

## Act I thematic movement

Carrying a father becomes carrying an inheritance.

# Sequence I - The Last Morning

## Purpose

Introduce Solomon entirely through his Vancouver house. Establish Malik, Ade, and Kai through behavior before the film explains their relationships.

## Chronology and geography

Pre-dawn, Solomon's Vancouver house, morning of the funeral.

Solomon is already cremated. His remains are in the single brass urn.

## Scene list

### Scene 1 - Kitchen and living room

Slugline: `INT. SOLOMON'S HOUSE - PRE-DAWN`

Movement:

1. Malik has entered early and activated the ordinary morning systems.
2. The house portrait reveals Solomon through books, pantry, plants, immigration manuals, prayer beads, and maintenance.
3. Ade arrives and tests Malik with humor.
4. Kai arrives and identifies repeatable errors and missing objects.
5. The brothers confront the empty staircase.
6. Malik takes the urn.
7. Ade offers his hands.
8. Malik refuses and assigns him the flowers.
9. They leave.

## Character matrix

### Malik

Objective: Make the morning function without error.

Avoidance: The hospital and the possibility that competence did not save Solomon.

Behavior: Checks burners, stops the faulty clock, aligns coffee, folds towel, controls departure, cleans the urn.

Pressure: Ade's jokes, Kai's questions, the empty staircase, offered help.

Turn: He refuses help with the urn and chooses burden over vulnerability.

Residue: He enters the funeral already overloaded.

### Ade

Objective: Restart emotional circulation between the brothers.

Avoidance: His own unstructured grief.

Behavior: Jokes, notices responses, closes the refrigerator before the mustard memory takes over.

Pressure: The battery joke fails.

Turn: He offers physical help instead of another joke.

Residue: Malik's refusal confirms that humor is not enough.

### Kai

Objective: Determine what in Solomon's house no longer fits.

Avoidance: Accepting that the father cannot provide a final explanation.

Behavior: Measures the clock error, checks the camcorder battery, searches the drawer, tests the photograph angle, reads the dust outline.

Pressure: Malik repeatedly ends the answer.

Turn: He leaves the photograph crooked after understanding its function.

Residue: The carving, missing battery, and empty shelf become unresolved human evidence.

## Knowledge movement

Audience learns:

- Solomon was culturally and bureaucratically divided
- Malik performs love through control
- Ade performs connection through humor
- Kai reads systems through pattern
- something was removed from the shelf
- the camcorder battery is missing
- the brass carving mattered enough to hide

No one learns anything supernatural.

## Cultural behavior

- Efik Bible repaired rather than displayed
- Nigerian pantry integrated into a Canadian kitchen
- prayer beads and walking stick treated as daily possessions
- immigration manuals reveal the long administrative labor of diaspora life

## Director's notebook

### Camera

Begin with fixed domestic frames and allow characters to enter Solomon's arrangements. Avoid horror grammar. The staircase is filmed as an absence, not a threat.

Kai's camera remains a prop and cognitive tool. Do not show camera POV that reveals hidden material.

### Light and color

Vancouver pre-dawn blue against warm kitchen practicals. Brass should carry warmth without glow.

### Sound

Kettle, pipes, refrigerator, clock, drawer contents, fabric. The house is mechanically ordinary.

### Performance

Do not announce grief. Let it appear in interrupted actions: Ade closing the refrigerator, Malik not drinking, Kai reopening the drawer.

### Unforgettable image

The empty staircase in a house prepared for Solomon to come down.

### Exit pressure

Malik walks out carrying the father and refusing shared weight.

## Integrity verdict

Chronology: pass

Character specificity: pass

Cultural integrity: pass

Supernatural causality: pass - no impossible event

Could this belong to another film? No. The scene's identity depends on Solomon's Oku household and the brothers' distinct relationships to his systems.

# Sequence II - The Funeral

## Purpose

Move grief into public space, introduce Bode through restraint, begin sharing Solomon's weight, and reveal the erased sister as a human wound.

## Chronology and geography

Morning to afternoon, Mountain View Cemetery memorial lawn and reception room in Vancouver.

The urn is present but not interred.

## Scene list

### Scene 2 - Urn-centered ceremony

Slugline: `EXT. MOUNTAIN VIEW CEMETERY - MEMORIAL LAWN - MORNING`

Movement:

1. The urn sits beneath a white canopy.
2. Malik monitors order of service.
3. Ade performs condolences.
4. Kai stands without using the camera.
5. Ordinary microphone feedback overwhelms Kai.
6. Ade regulates him through breath and eye contact.
7. Bode arrives from Nigeria and waits.
8. Bode asks whether Solomon's death was peaceful.
9. Malik lies.
10. Bode offers one childhood memory.
11. Malik nearly loses his grip on the urn.
12. He admits his fear of another mistake.
13. Bode asks for one side.

### Scene 3 - Reception

Slugline: `INT. MOUNTAIN VIEW CEMETERY - RECEPTION ROOM - LATER`

Movement:

1. Contradictory public memories of Solomon register in one compressed movement.
2. Kai finds the scratched photograph.
3. Ade verifies the damage was intentional.
4. Bode identifies the woman emotionally, not by name.
5. Bode announces the will meeting.

Pacing rule: Enter on the food and overlapping claims, find the photograph immediately, and leave. The reception is a hinge, not a second funeral.

### Scene 4 - Transfer to car

Slugline: `EXT. MOUNTAIN VIEW CEMETERY - PARKING AREA - AFTERNOON`

Movement:

1. Bode and Malik carry the urn beneath one small umbrella.
2. Malik allows Bode only one side.
3. The family takes Solomon home.

## Character matrix

### Malik

Objective: Execute the funeral correctly.

Avoidance: Admitting he was absent or late at the end.

Behavior: Folds programme, monitors choir, takes urn first, grips until his fingers whiten.

Pressure: Kai's distress occurs outside Malik's attention; Bode asks the one question Malik cannot answer honestly; the urn shifts.

Turn: Says, "I'm trying not to make another mistake for him," and gives Bode one side.

Residue: Control has become shareable but not surrendered.

### Ade

Objective: Keep other mourners and his brothers functioning.

Avoidance: Being a mourner rather than a helper.

Behavior: Receives condolences, notices Kai, uses breath rather than humor.

Pressure: Public performance leaves him empty when no one watches.

Turn: Identifies intentional damage in the photograph without joking.

Residue: Begins treating the family mystery as harm rather than atmosphere.

### Kai

Objective: Endure the public ritual and identify what the family display omits.

Avoidance: Directly filming or confronting Solomon's remains.

Behavior: Keeps camera down, regulates after feedback, studies photo emulsion and background object.

Pressure: Sensory overload; Bode's incomplete answer.

Turn: Keeps the damaged photograph as a question.

Residue: Carries the first evidence of an erased family member.

### Bode

Objective: Arrive without making Solomon's funeral about his arrival.

Avoidance: Public reckoning with the brother he lost and the sister they failed.

Behavior: Waits, accepts Malik's lie, gives a childhood memory, supports the urn, sets a boundary around the sister's name.

Pressure: Malik's admission and Kai's direct question.

Turn: Moves from observer to participant by taking one side of the urn.

Residue: Must bring the brothers to the will without selling them the journey.

## Knowledge movement

The brothers learn:

- Bode knew a version of Solomon that was less controlled
- the scratched woman was loved and harmed
- the scratched woman and the brass carving are connected to the Calabar house
- a will meeting is imminent

They do not learn:

- her name
- that she is Solomon and Bode's younger sister
- shrine mythology
- any supernatural rule

## Cultural behavior

- Nigerian choir and funeral food exist within a Vancouver diaspora gathering
- church and professional communities claim different Solomons
- Bode's elder role emerges through waiting and practical help, not a title speech

## Director's notebook

### Camera

Keep the urn central but do not fetishize it. During feedback, subjective sound may narrow while the image remains objective enough to show Ade entering Kai's field.

At the reception, photograph grooves should be visible through angle and light, not an insert designed as a jump scare.

### Light and color

Wet greys under the canopy; warmer but flatter fluorescence in the reception room. The scratched emulsion catches white light.

### Sound

Feedback must have an identifiable technical source and stop when equipment is switched off. The choir continuing acoustically restores human sound.

### Performance

Bode does not signal that he knows the mythology. He signals that he knows the cost of the family.

### Unforgettable image

Malik and Bode carrying one urn under an umbrella too small for both.

### Exit pressure

The will transforms the funeral's ending into a beginning.

## Integrity verdict

Chronology: pass

Character specificity: pass

Cultural integrity: pass at working level

Supernatural causality: pass - feedback is ordinary; photograph is physically damaged

Could this belong to another film? No. Shared urn weight and the scratched Oku sister connect grief directly to inheritance.

# Sequence III - The Will and Decision

## Purpose

Give the dead father's request practical force without allowing him to narrate the movie. Make Calabar the brothers' voluntary decision.

## Chronology and geography

Next morning, Solomon's Vancouver house.

## Scene list

### Scene 5 - Will reading

Slugline: `INT. SOLOMON'S HOUSE - DINING ROOM - MORNING`

Movement:

1. Lawyer Maya Patel brings one file and one letter.
2. The property is offered jointly without debt or penalty.
3. Solomon's request is read: "I need you to take me home. All three."
4. Maya clarifies river boundary and right of refusal.
5. Maya leaves the family to decide.

### Scene 6 - Decision

Sluglines:

- `INT. SOLOMON'S HOUSE - DINING ROOM - MOMENTS LATER`

Movement:

1. Kai connects house, photograph, carving, and silence through observable evidence.
2. Bode confirms the woman was his and Solomon's sister and is dead, but stops before explaining her death.
3. Malik votes no in substance.
4. Bode refuses to decide.
5. Ade identifies Solomon's arrangement.
6. Ade votes yes to preserve the brotherhood.
7. Kai votes yes.
8. Malik chooses to go together and stay together.
9. A seat-belt chime carries the decision directly into descent over Calabar.

## Character matrix

### Malik

Objective: Prevent Solomon from controlling the family after death.

Avoidance: A journey where his competence may fail and his father's history may worsen.

Behavior: Tests the only legal condition, demands Bode decide, and makes staying together his condition for travel.

Pressure: The gift is voluntary; he cannot frame refusal as protection from legal harm.

Turn: Chooses the brothers rather than Solomon's instruction.

Residue: "Stay together" becomes a rule Act II can test.

### Ade

Objective: Prevent a permanent split.

Avoidance: Naming how easily the brothers could become separate families.

Behavior: Reads the letter aloud, identifies the arrangement, reasons through relational consequence.

Pressure: Malik says no; Kai will go.

Turn: Votes yes for brotherhood, not obedience.

Residue: His moral authority is established beneath the humor.

### Kai

Objective: Follow the concealed human pattern to its source.

Avoidance: Accepting Bode's boundaries around information.

Behavior: Compares carving to photograph and names the family's repeated refusal.

Pressure: Malik dismisses pattern as estate administration.

Turn: States he will not inherit silence as explanation.

Residue: The screenplay later reveals that the evidence travelled with him; no packing scene is required.

### Bode

Objective: Preserve the brothers' agency.

Avoidance: Repeating Solomon's controlling form of protection.

Behavior: Confirms the sister's relationship, limits what he will say about her death, and refuses a deciding vote.

Pressure: Malik demands certainty.

Turn: Says Solomon did not appoint him their father.

Residue: He becomes guide rather than decision-maker.

## Knowledge movement

The brothers learn:

- the Calabar property belongs to all three jointly if accepted
- refusal carries no debt or punishment
- the river boundary is Solomon's requested destination
- the scratched woman was Solomon and Bode's sister
- she is dead

They do not learn:

- the shrine
- bloodline maintenance
- the Entity
- the sister's death

## Director's notebook

### Camera

Frame the family around the table without making the composition perfectly symmetrical. The urn remains visible but not centered in every shot. The decision must belong to faces and hands, not legal inserts.

### Sound

Paper and a refrigerator opening. On Malik's condition that they stay together, the room gives way to a seat-belt chime and the pilot's landing call.

### Performance

Maya's humanity comes from what she declines to dramatize. Ade's vote must lose any comic cadence before the final release line.

### Unforgettable image

Solomon's urn waits on the sideboard while his sons decide whether to carry him to a river none of them knows.

### Exit pressure

The emotional choice cuts directly to consequence: Calabar rises beneath the aircraft.

## Integrity verdict

Chronology: pass

Legal simplicity: pass at screenplay level; Nigerian property mechanics remain off-screen and require legal review only if later plot depends on title transfer

Character specificity: pass

Supernatural causality: pass

Could this belong to another film? No. The choice grows from the specific erased sister, Oku land, urn, and three-brother structure.

# Sequence IV - Arrival

## Purpose

Cross geography without spending pages on logistics. Rejoin the family at the instant Canada gives way to Calabar, then transfer practical authority from Malik to Bode.

## Chronology and geography

Several days later, with all intermediate travel elided:

- domestic flight descending into Calabar
- Margaret Ekpo International Airport, Calabar
- road toward Oku family land

The cut assumes the ordinary work of passports, packing, international transit, and lawful carriage of the urn. None of it deserves dramatic space unless it changes a relationship.

## Scene list

### Scene 7 - Descent

The seat-belt chime begins over the decision in Vancouver. On the cut, green and water gather beneath the wing. The same urn is visible beneath Malik's seat. Wheels strike the Calabar runway.

### Scene 8 - Calabar arrival

A porter begins handling Ade's bag. Malik reacts. Bode resolves the interaction through calm elder authority. No bag is lost and the exchange lasts seconds.

### Scene 9 - Road home

Bode reveals Solomon raced bicycles on the road and had a childhood before Canada. The vehicle leaves asphalt for red earth.

## Character matrix

### Malik

Objective: Regain control as soon as the family leaves the aircraft.

Avoidance: Reliance on Bode.

Turn: Bode resolves the bag interaction before Malik can turn uncertainty into conflict; Malik follows.

Residue: His authority is now contextual rather than absolute.

### Ade

Objective: Reduce travel pressure without making Nigeria or grief the joke.

Turn: His humor turns Calabar heat and Solomon's unknown childhood into shared family rhythm.

### Kai

Objective: Record the new environment without turning it into spectacle.

Turn: Films ordinary life from the road rather than searching for an omen.

### Bode

Objective: Bring the family through systems he understands.

Turn: His guidance becomes concrete the moment the terminal doors open.

## Cultural behavior

- airport workers remain individuals, not threat
- Pidgin is brief and situational
- Bode's elder authority is recognized without a confrontation
- Calabar appears through ordinary life and scale

## Director's notebook

### Camera

Use one decisive sound cut instead of a travel montage. The first geographic image is the Calabar approach, followed immediately by human density and legible work.

### Sound

Seat-belt chime, pilot landing call, wheels striking runway, then names and traffic across physical space. Heat has no sound effect; show its pressure on bodies.

### Unforgettable image

The brothers step into Calabar heat still dressed with Vancouver precision.

### Exit pressure

Bode says "Home" as the tires leave asphalt.

## Integrity verdict

Chronology: pass

Urn continuity: pass

Cultural integrity: pass at working level; Pidgin line requires regional reader

Supernatural causality: pass - no anomaly

Could this belong to another film? It is made specific by the authority transfer, Solomon bicycle memory, and single-urn journey.

# Sequence V - Homecoming

## Purpose

Let the brothers belong before frightening them. Humanize Solomon through family contradiction and introduce Eno as a living force.

## Chronology and geography

Late afternoon through next morning, Oku family compound outside Calabar.

## Scene list

### Scene 10 - Compound arrival

Movement:

1. Compound introduced as contemporary and multigenerational.
2. Bode greets Eno with `Mọkọm`; she answers `Ekọm do`.
3. Eno corrects the brothers until they offer the same respectful greeting.
4. She reads Solomon in Malik's face.
5. She identifies Ade's mouth as family continuity.
6. She recognizes Kai's looking without mystifying it.
7. She acknowledges Solomon at the urn.

### Scene 11 - Front room

Movement:

1. Solomon's urn enters a contemporary, occupied family house.
2. Malik tests the table before setting it down.
3. Eno punctures his control without humiliating him.

### Scene 12 - Family dinner

Movement:

1. Eno feeds before answering.
2. Malik negotiates with food.
3. Ade claims cooking confidence and is corrected.
4. Mango story reconstructs Solomon as a boy.
5. Boiled-water story releases real laughter.
6. Malik asks what happened.
7. Eno redirects to the land walk without false ominousness.

### Scene 13 - Brothers' room

Movement:

1. Brothers process the ordinary shock of a funny young Solomon.
2. Kai notes the sister's continued omission.
3. Malik promises "tomorrow" as a real time, not a permanent deferral.

### Scene 14 - Breakfast

Movement:

1. Eno greets the brothers as a group with `Emesiere`; they answer `Emesiere nde`.
2. Eggs, plantain, bread, tea, and leftover Afang establish household abundance and continuity.
3. Eno wins the egg negotiation.
4. Bode announces the land walk and possible continuation to the river.

## Character matrix

### Malik

Objective: Enter the household without losing functional control.

Pressure: Eno touches his face, feeds him, and identifies his negotiations.

Turn: Laughs before monitoring himself and accepts Eno's food verdict.

Residue: Calabar begins giving him a father he cannot manage.

### Ade

Objective: Create connection without being dismissed as culturally foreign.

Pressure: Eno corrects greeting and cooking confidence.

Turn: Laughs without performing and receives correction as belonging.

Residue: Humor can now connect him to family history rather than only protect the brothers.

### Kai

Objective: Identify omissions in the stories.

Pressure: Everyone supplies contradictory Solomon stories but no sister.

Turn: Accepts one night's delay after Malik turns "tomorrow" into a genuine promise.

Residue: His unresolved question enters the land walk.

### Bode

Objective: Re-enter family life without allowing the Vancouver mission to dominate it.

Turn: Participates in contradictory stories rather than controlling the narrative.

### Eno

Objective: Establish the brothers as family before history is discussed.

Turn: Converts strangers-with-blood into nephews through greeting, touch, food, and teasing.

Residue: Her authority can later challenge Bode's containment logic.

## Cultural and language behavior

- The late-afternoon Efik greeting is an act of family reintegration, not decorative vocabulary.
- The brothers' repetition of `Mọkọm` shows respect without pretending fluency.
- The morning call-and-response shows that they listened and can improve.
- Eno uses Nigerian English with the brothers because it is shared; her Efik marks belonging and hierarchy.
- Pidgin remains available for informal or public contexts but is not forced into every family line.
- Register changes by relationship, not nationality.
- No dialogue is spelled phonetically to simulate accent.
- Shrine terminology is explicitly deferred until the regulator mythology receives specialist cultural review.

## Director's notebook

### Camera

The compound should be filmed through use, not establishing-shot tourism. Let people cross frames and interrupt compositions. Keep the urn visible at dinner without centering every conversation around it.

### Production design

Old carved wood beside charging cables, water tanks, bicycles, solar panels, plastic chairs, family photographs, and daily work.

### Sound

Overlapping talk, dishes, children, distant generator. No drum motif.

### Performance

The mango and boiled-water stories should feel contested in real time. No actor delivers "backstory." They argue ownership of memory.

### Unforgettable image

The dead father outnumbered by living, contradictory versions of himself at the table.

### Exit pressure

The river request becomes a physical walk through Solomon's childhood land.

## Integrity verdict

Chronology: pass

Eno geography: pass - first appearance is Calabar

Cultural integrity: researched working pass. Greeting forms are grounded; performance cadence, family-specific usage, and food service still require an Efik/Calabar reader.

Supernatural causality: pass - no warning, omen, spider, or impossible event

Could this belong to another film? No. Eno's family authority, Solomon-specific stories, and Oku household make the sequence proprietary.

# Sequence VI - The Land and the Shrine

## Purpose

Make Kai's core character engine cause the supernatural inciting incident. Reveal the regulator through action, not exposition.

## Chronology and geography

Morning, Oku family land, on a walk toward the river.

The urn remains with Malik, Ade, and Bode when Kai leaves the path.

## Scene list

### Scene 15 - Departure from compound

Malik and Ade carry the urn together. Eno sends everybody back before dark.

### Scene 16 - Family land

Bode maps lived boundaries against legal title. Kai recognizes the carving's imperfect family mark on a boundary stone and asks whether it belonged to the erased sister. Bode tells him to put it away. Solomon's planted tree proves a human past.

### Scene 17 - Old palm track

Bode warns the brothers:

- stay on his path
- sound bends around water and trees
- look for a person before following a voice
- distance deceives under dense canopy

The warning is practical and inherited, not occult theater.

### Scene 18 - Boundary inconsistency

A fallen limb has exposed one older marked stone on the wrong side of the path. Kai steps off to photograph the object Bode did not identify.

### Scene 19 - Ravine

The slope gives way after two steps. Kai protects his camera in the fall and discovers that the exposed stone is the upper corner of the shrine.

### Scene 20 - Shrine breach

Movement:

1. Stone and tree are revealed as one regulatory structure.
2. Imperfect cuts link the carving and shrine through human workmanship, not an exact geometric key.
3. He identifies the maintained but neglected red-thread seal.
4. He finds the intentional opening in the knot.
5. He works it.
6. The brittle thread breaks.
7. He exposes the talisman.
8. He photographs it.
9. He removes it intentionally.
10. Natural sound stops.
11. Tactile inherited fragments arrive without explaining identities or chronology.
12. The land produces one deep beat.
13. Kai raises the talisman toward its recess.
14. Malik calls again.
15. Kai closes his hand and keeps the talisman without explaining himself.
16. The first spider crosses the thread and stops on the broken loop.

## Character matrix

### Malik

Objective: Carry Solomon safely toward the river.

Avoidance: Trusting the land or Kai's independence.

Turn before the split: Shares the urn with Ade and Bode.

Residue: His rule of staying together fails at the first land threshold.

### Ade

Objective: Keep the walk human and the brothers connected.

Turn before the split: Carries the urn alone for part of the climb, converting jokes about responsibility into actual responsibility.

Residue: He will have to decide how to respond when Kai's mistake becomes dangerous.

### Kai

Objective: Find the human source of a family mark and the omitted sister's connection to it.

Avoidance: Accepting Bode's refusal as the final answer.

Behavior: Compares, asks, photographs, steps off, opens, removes, nearly restores, keeps.

Pressure: Bode's warning, the fall, the visible opportunity to climb away, and Malik calling from above.

Turn: Holds the talisman within an inch of restoration, then closes his hand.

Residue: Responsibility, unexplained sensory memory, and the Entity's recognition. Shame must emerge from what Kai does next, not be supplied in advance.

### Bode

Objective: Bring Solomon's sons across the land without activating old danger.

Avoidance: Explaining the full inherited system before they reach the appropriate family context.

Behavior: Shows boundaries, gives practical warning, monitors urn transfer.

Pressure: Kai disappears.

Turn: Fear enters his voice.

Residue: Bode must decide whether truth or procedure comes first.

## Hidden mythology

- the imperfect three-cut mark belongs to the human maintenance system
- the carving is a portable representation, not a map or key
- repeated red-thread repair shows active maintenance across generations
- neglect, not a single villain, allowed the seal to become brittle
- bloodline contact makes the breach legible to the Entity
- the talisman is a functional component, not a souvenir

## Resonance content

The resonance must remain fragmentary:

- an older palm beneath Kai's
- red thread cutting across both hands
- river water at chest height
- brass striking stone beneath water

No face, name, death, maintenance history, guilt, or causal chronology is supplied. Solomon is never shown alive. The images acquire meaning only when later dramatic action earns it.

## Director's notebook

### Camera

The path should be spatially legible before Kai leaves it. The audience must understand he makes a choice and can turn back.

The off-path stone is exposed by a fallen limb and discovered through real optics. No camera anomaly, rotating-marker map, or supernatural lure.

The shrine reveal should be slow enough to read construction: masonry, roots, repeated repairs, recess, thread.

### Light and color

Natural green and red earth. The shrine is not under a theatrical shaft of light. Its visibility comes from Kai reaching the ravine floor.

Brass shifts from warm household material to cold functional component at contact.

### Sound

Build a dense natural field before removing it completely. The silence must be the first undeniable impossibility in the film.

The deep beat is felt in root and teeth. It is not score announcing a villain.

### Performance

Kai must show the difference between:

- curiosity
- hesitation
- choice
- shock
- the near-restoration
- the second choice to keep what he removed

The audience should be able to blame and understand him simultaneously.

### Unforgettable image

The spider crossing the snapped red thread and stopping at the failed knot.

### Exit pressure

Act II must begin with an open regulator, missing Kai, unscattered ashes, and Bode's first fear.

## Integrity verdict

Chronology: pass

Character causality: pass - Kai's pattern engine causes the breach

Mythology: pass at Act I level

Supernatural phase: pass - first impossibility follows removal of talisman

Cultural integrity: working pass; the shrine's final design, maintenance practice, spider-deity terminology, and red-thread usage require specialist review

Could this belong to another film? No. The scene unites Oku bloodline maintenance, Solomon's erased sister, Kai's refusal of family silence, an imperfect family mark, and the balance-correcting Entity.
