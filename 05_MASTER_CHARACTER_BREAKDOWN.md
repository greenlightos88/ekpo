# EKPO MASTER CHARACTER BREAKDOWN

Version: 1.0 - Canonical Character Control Document

Status: Working canon through Act II, Sequence VII

## Purpose

This file is the repository's primary authority for character psychology, behavior, decision-making, relationship dynamics, performance logic, and anti-drift rules.

It exists so that later sequences do not flatten the cast into functions, repeat completed beats, contradict established behavior, or force a character to make a decision merely because the plot requires it.

This is not a collection of biographies. It is a dramatic operating system.

For each character, the document defines:

- what the character wants
- what the character is protecting
- what the character cannot admit
- how the character regulates emotion
- how the character makes decisions under pressure
- what changes that decision process
- how the character speaks, moves, and withholds
- what the supernatural may eventually exploit
- what must never drift

The character engines in this file are canonical. Later plot outcomes remain provisional until they pass the project's full sequence integrity process.

# Authority and Maintenance Protocol

When character sources conflict, use this hierarchy:

1. The user's latest explicit correction.
2. `00_CANON.md` for chronology, geography, object continuity, and supernatural phase law.
3. This file for character psychology, decision-making, behavior, voice, and relationships.
4. `02_MASTER_BIBLE.md` for thematic, motif, and feature architecture.
5. The current screenplay for executed behavior.
6. Development documents and older source PDFs for recoverable material only.

When a later sequence changes a character in a lasting way, update this file in the same commit as the screenplay and canon. Do not allow screenplay pages and character canon to diverge.

A character change is lasting if it alters any of the following:

- the character's governing fear
- the character's decision hierarchy
- the character's relationship to another principal character
- the character's understanding of Solomon
- the character's relationship to the inherited obligation
- the character's response to the Entity
- the character's ability to trust perception, memory, or family

No character is "locked" merely because one scene works. Character continuity is a moving ledger.

# Psychological Method and Diagnostic Caution

The psychological panels are writing and performance tools, not medical diagnoses.

The film must never use a psychiatric label as shorthand for:

- supernatural sensitivity
- danger
- spiritual knowledge
- eccentricity
- violence
- unreliability
- moral weakness

Shared supernatural events are not evidence of psychosis. Kai's pattern sensitivity is not automatically autism. Malik's checking is not automatically obsessive-compulsive disorder. Ade's humor is not automatically pathology. Bode's inherited caution is not proof of a personality disorder.

If a future draft intentionally makes a character neurodivergent or clinically diagnosable, that identity must be built through history, functional patterns, relationships, strengths, difficulties, and lived perspective. It cannot be added retroactively to explain a plot mechanism.

The panels below identify plausible emotional and behavioral lenses only.

# Ensemble Architecture

The Oku family is organized around different methods of surviving uncertainty.

| Character | Surface strategy | Hidden need | Primary danger | Long-form movement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Malik | Control the environment | Proof that his care is sufficient | Confusing protection with possession | Control toward consent |
| Ade | Keep emotion circulating | Belonging without performance | Confusing usefulness with worth | Performance toward intrinsic worth |
| Kai | Make the hidden pattern legible | A father and family history he can understand | Confusing emotional accuracy with truth | Certainty-seeking toward chosen uncertainty |
| Bode | Respect inherited boundaries | To prevent the past from repeating | Confusing containment with protection | Containment toward relational responsibility |
| Eno | Make care materially unavoidable | A family capable of facing truth and continuing to live | Carrying ordinary life while men preserve silence | Practical care toward active truth-telling |
| Solomon | Arrange people and systems from absence | To protect his sons from what he could not repair | Turning protection into another inheritance of silence | Mystery toward human contradiction |
| Younger sister | Presently exists through erasure and memory | Restoration to full human personhood | Being reduced to a clue or a victim | Erased absence toward acknowledged consequence |
| Entity | Correct damaged balance | Restoration according to an inhuman standard | Treating descendants as continuous with ancestral failure | Recognition toward attachment, then correction |

The brothers must remain a three-part system:

- Malik organizes survival.
- Ade regulates connection.
- Kai identifies what the system excludes.

If any brother can be removed without fundamentally changing how the other two function, the ensemble has drifted.

# MALIK OKU

## Canonical Identity

- Age: 36
- Family position: Eldest son
- Act I geography: Vancouver to Calabar
- Story function: Protector, organizer, and control center of the sibling unit
- Core engine: He fights entropy

## Actor-Facing Essence

Malik turns care into management because management gives him evidence that he loved correctly.

He does not clean, check, arrange, or plan because the screenplay needs a controlling eldest brother. He acts because preventable error feels morally unforgivable. Every correction is an attempt to keep catastrophe from acquiring his name.

His competence is real. It has protected people. The tragedy is not that Malik is controlling because control never works. The tragedy is that control works often enough for him to mistake it for love itself.

## Public Mask

Capable. Exact. Difficult to surprise. The person who has already read the instructions, checked the route, and noticed what everyone forgot.

He would rather be resented for taking over than forgiven for failing to act.

## Private Psychological Engine

Malik equates:

- vigilance with love
- usefulness with worth
- anticipation with responsibility
- another person's safety with his own moral performance

His deepest fear is not simply losing someone. It is being responsible for a loss he believes he could have prevented.

His grief over Solomon is therefore contaminated by self-judgment. He is not only mourning his father. He is auditing his own conduct around the death.

## Conscious Want

Complete Solomon's funeral and estate obligations without further damage to the family.

## Unconscious Need

Learn that responsibility can be shared and that another person's autonomy is not evidence of Malik's failure.

## Governing Fear

If Malik stops monitoring the people he loves, something irreversible will happen and everyone will know he should have prevented it.

## Governing Shame

He believes there was a moment around Solomon's death when he was not present, not fast enough, or not useful enough. The exact facts may develop, but the shame is canonical.

## Attachment Strategy

Malik attaches through service, correction, preparation, and proximity.

He rarely asks, "What do you need?"

He notices a problem and solves it before the other person can answer.

This makes him dependable and invasive at the same time.

## Core Contradiction

The brother most committed to keeping the family together is often the brother least able to let the others choose how they stand beside him.

## Strengths

- reliable under ordinary pressure
- observant about logistics and physical risk
- able to make difficult practical decisions
- willing to carry burdens without applause
- persistent when other people freeze
- capable of decisive action
- deeply loyal

## Blind Spots

- mistakes compliance for safety
- treats uncertainty as a defect rather than a condition
- overvalues what can be measured
- hears disagreement as withdrawal from the family
- notices external disorder before internal distress
- can make another person's fear about his own ability to fix it

## Psychological Panel

Malik presents with high vigilance, inflated responsibility, control-based coping, guilt, and anxiety around preventable harm.

Possible fiction-writing lenses include:

- trauma-related hyperarousal
- complicated grief
- responsibility-focused obsessive-compulsive features
- rigid coping under uncertainty

The text does not establish a diagnosis. His behaviors should not be written as quirky compulsions detached from threatened objects. If he checks something, the writer must know what catastrophe he is trying to prevent.

## Decision-Making Engine

### Default sequence

1. Scan for risk.
2. Identify the variable he can control.
3. Assign himself responsibility.
4. Act before consensus can slow him.
5. Interpret resistance as increased danger.
6. Tighten control.

### What he prioritizes

1. Immediate physical safety
2. Keeping the brothers in one place
3. Preventing irreversible action
4. Completing the family's practical obligation
5. Emotional truth

This hierarchy is deliberately flawed. Emotional truth arrives late because Malik considers it useless unless it changes what can be done.

### What persuades him

Malik is not persuaded by abstract appeals to openness. He changes course when:

- a concrete action preserves family unity better than his preferred plan
- another person demonstrates competence
- he sees that control itself is causing separation
- a choice is framed as responsibility shared among the living
- someone waits without humiliating him

Bode's hand beneath the urn works because it offers support without declaring Malik incapable.

### What makes him dangerous

When Malik believes time is running out, he may remove choice from the people he is trying to save.

### Growth decision

His mature decision process must become:

1. Identify danger.
2. State what he fears.
3. Ask what the other person wants.
4. Share available information.
5. Act with consent where time permits.
6. Accept that protection cannot guarantee outcome.

## Behavioral Vocabulary

Use:

- checks burners, surfaces, locks, documents, routes, footing, load-bearing objects
- corrects a fold, angle, time, placement, or procedure
- removes hazards before anyone notices
- touches a brother's clothing to fix it rather than to comfort him
- carries weight before asking who should
- goes quiet when control fails

Avoid:

- generic pacing
- repetitive handwashing or counting with no dramatic object
- constant clenched jaws
- explaining that he "needs control"
- making every scene prove he is emotionally repressed in the same way

Each control behavior must evolve or disappear after it has completed its function.

## Voice and Silence

Malik uses:

- compressed declarative sentences
- practical questions
- conditional language
- corrections
- exact nouns

He avoids metaphor unless angry enough to lose precision.

He often turns emotion into logistics:

- "When?"
- "Who signed this?"
- "Where is Kai?"
- "We stay together."

His silence is not emptiness. It is an attempt to prevent uncontrolled feeling from becoming uncontrolled action.

## Physical Performance

Malik's emotion should first appear as an action performed too quickly:

- a crease smoothed
- an urn gripped
- a surface tested twice
- a route corrected
- a hand withdrawn after help is accepted

The actor should not signal fragility beneath every competent moment. Malik's loss of control matters only if his control has often been credible.

## Relationship Engines

### With Ade

Malik relies on Ade to keep emotional life moving, then dismisses humor as unserious. Ade can reach him, but Malik resents needing that access.

Malik's growth requires recognizing Ade as a moral decision-maker, not merely the brother who makes difficult rooms tolerable.

### With Kai

Malik experiences Kai's curiosity as preventable exposure.

Kai experiences Malik's certainty as another locked door.

Malik must eventually protect Kai without converting Kai into property. Kai must eventually accept care without treating every boundary as concealment.

### With Bode

Bode is the first elder whose authority Malik cannot reduce to incompetence or posthumous control.

Their conflict is not simply modern versus traditional. Both men use rules to prevent loss. The difference is which system each trusts.

### With Eno

Eno bypasses Malik's managerial identity through touch, food, correction, and family memory. She treats him as a nephew before he can establish himself as the competent eldest son.

### With Solomon

Malik inherited Solomon's belief that preparation can control emotional consequence. His anger at Solomon is partly recognition.

## Act I Movement

1. Restores order in Solomon's house.
2. Refuses Ade's help with the urn.
3. Misses Kai's distress while monitoring the funeral.
4. Lies that Solomon's death was peaceful.
5. Admits he is trying not to make another mistake for Solomon.
6. Allows Bode to take one side of the urn.
7. Resists Calabar as another posthumous arrangement.
8. Agrees only when the journey becomes a choice among the brothers.
9. Enters Calabar, where his competence becomes contextual.
10. Shares the urn's weight on the family land.
11. Ends Act I calling for Kai after the rule "stay together" has failed.

## Long-Form Trajectory

Thematic compass, not locked plot:

> Control toward consent.

Malik must learn that protection is not possession.

His final mature choices should preserve commitment without erasing another person's agency. The strongest version of his arc does not make him passive. It makes him capable of asking before acting and capable of remaining present when an answer is not the one he wants.

## Post-Trigger Vulnerability

The Entity may eventually exploit:

- lateness
- separation
- failed rescue
- counterfeit requests for help
- evidence that his rules caused harm
- impossible choices in which saving one brother appears to abandon another

This exploitation cannot begin at full intensity. The Entity must first observe Malik's control behavior on screen.

## Anti-Drift Locks

Malik must never become:

- a generic authoritarian eldest brother
- emotionally stupid
- incompetent to make Kai seem perceptive
- correct about every danger
- wrong about every danger
- a collection of obsessive mannerisms
- violent merely because he is frustrated
- cured of control by one confession

## Scene Integrity Questions

- What threatened object activates Malik's control?
- What useful result does his control produce?
- What relational cost does it produce?
- Did he ask or assume?
- Has this exact control beat already occurred?
- Does the scene move him toward greater consent or tighter possession?

# ADE OKU

## Canonical Identity

- Age: 32
- Family position: Middle son
- Act I geography: Vancouver to Calabar
- Story function: Humorist, emotional regulator, and social pressure valve
- Core engine: Humor is emotional CPR

## Actor-Facing Essence

Ade monitors emotional circulation. He jokes when a room is about to stop breathing.

His humor is not a genre obligation. It is an attachment strategy, diagnostic tool, and emergency response. He can feel when people are withdrawing from one another and tries to restart contact before silence becomes separation.

The cost is that Ade can disappear inside the function he performs for everyone else.

## Public Mask

Adaptable. Warm. Socially fluent. Apparently less burdened than Malik and less consumed than Kai.

He makes other people feel that surviving the room is possible.

## Private Psychological Engine

Ade fears his place in the family depends on what he does to other people's emotions.

He equates:

- laughter with restored connection
- usefulness with belonging
- performance with safety
- other people's relief with proof that he matters

His hidden terror is not that people will dislike him. It is that if he stops entertaining, translating, or softening, nobody will need him in the room.

## Conscious Want

Keep the brothers connected long enough to complete Solomon's request without destroying one another.

## Unconscious Need

Learn that he belongs when he is frightened, angry, silent, or unable to rescue the mood.

## Governing Fear

If he stops carrying the emotional atmosphere, the family will fracture and discover he contributes nothing else.

## Governing Shame

He worries that his apparent ease means his grief is shallow, or that other people see him as the brother least capable of seriousness.

Being told he has Solomon's laugh wounds him because it turns his most useful trait into an inheritance he does not control.

## Attachment Strategy

Ade attaches through:

- humor
- attention
- social adaptation
- translation between personalities
- making another person feel less exposed

He asks what the room needs before asking what he needs.

## Core Contradiction

The brother who creates the most connection is often the least visible inside that connection.

## Strengths

- high social perception
- rapid emotional diagnosis
- flexible communication
- capacity to reduce shame
- ability to see multiple positions
- moral clarity when family unity is at stake
- willingness to make himself ridiculous so someone else can recover

## Blind Spots

- mistakes reduced tension for resolved conflict
- can interrupt necessary silence
- hides anger until it sharpens unexpectedly
- may resent people for depending on the role he keeps volunteering to perform
- treats his own fear as a threat to group stability
- risks giving everyone else permission to feel while denying it to himself

## Psychological Panel

Ade's humor functions as avoidance, social intelligence, attachment strategy, and self-protection.

Possible fiction-writing lenses include:

- caretaking or fawn response
- complicated grief
- anxiety around relational abandonment
- identity insecurity organized around usefulness
- stress-related dissociation only if later scenes deliberately establish it

No diagnosis is canonical. Missing time or perceptual disruption, if used later, must be evaluated against supernatural causality and cannot be casually medicalized.

## Decision-Making Engine

### Default sequence

1. Read the room.
2. Identify who is closest to withdrawing.
3. Offer humor, warmth, or a social bridge.
4. Watch whether connection returns.
5. Change tactics if the joke fails.
6. Postpone his own response.

### What he prioritizes

1. Keeping the brothers in relationship
2. Reducing shame
3. Preventing irreversible estrangement
4. Preserving someone's ability to choose
5. His own comfort

### What persuades him

Ade changes course when:

- a joke visibly injures instead of protects
- silence is the only honest form of presence
- someone identifies his performance without mocking it
- a decision would permanently split the brotherhood
- he is asked for companionship rather than emotional labor

### What makes him dangerous

When Ade is afraid of being unnecessary, he may keep a false peace alive after truth should have broken it.

### Growth decision

His mature decision process must become:

1. Read the room.
2. Identify his own feeling before treating everyone else's.
3. Decide whether the moment needs relief, truth, or silence.
4. Offer connection without performing invulnerability.
5. Allow another person to remain upset.
6. Stay even when he cannot repair the atmosphere.

## Humor Rules

Every joke must answer:

- Who is Ade protecting?
- What emotion is he interrupting?
- Is the joke for the room, one brother, or himself?
- Does it work?
- What does its success or failure reveal?

Ade should not sound like a screenwriter supplying punch lines.

He should sound like a man checking for a pulse.

When laughter becomes real, he briefly stops monitoring the room. Those moments are rare and valuable.

## Voice and Silence

Ade's speech is:

- quick but not careless
- relational
- responsive to other people's exact wording
- capable of turning an accusation into a shared joke
- more emotionally exact than he pretends

His serious lines should be simpler than his jokes.

When Ade stops joking, the audience should feel a support beam leave the room.

## Physical Performance

Ade often:

- watches the person who did not laugh
- uses touch casually to reduce another person's exposure
- places himself between brothers without announcing it
- performs ease with loose posture
- becomes still when a joke wounds him

His body should reveal the labor beneath apparent spontaneity.

## Relationship Engines

### With Malik

Ade tests whether Malik can still be reached. Malik treats him as unserious until Ade makes the clearest moral decision in the will sequence.

Ade must not exist merely to soften Malik. He should sometimes refuse the role Malik assigns him.

### With Kai

Ade notices Kai's distress earlier than Malik because he monitors people rather than systems.

He believes Kai more readily, but his desire to preserve connection may also make him vulnerable to a convincing imitation of Kai later.

### With Bode

Ade's humor tests Bode's flexibility. Bode's restrained responses tell Ade whether the elder can absorb irreverence without interpreting it as disrespect.

### With Eno

Eno recognizes his mouthiness as family continuity. Her teasing welcomes him without allowing performance to dominate the room.

### With Solomon

Ade inherited Solomon's laugh without inheriting a complete account of the man. His arc must examine whether resemblance is identity, obligation, or merely sound.

## Act I Movement

1. Uses jokes to test whether Malik remains reachable.
2. Fails to revive the room with the camcorder-battery joke.
3. Offers physical help with the urn.
4. Notices Kai's sensory overload before Malik does.
5. Is told he has Solomon's laugh and becomes conscious of his own smile.
6. Recognizes the photograph was deliberately damaged.
7. Names Solomon's arrangement without surrendering to it.
8. Chooses Calabar because he refuses permanent division.
9. Uses humor in Nigeria without making Nigeria the joke.
10. Laughs genuinely at Eno's table.
11. Carries real physical responsibility on the land.

## Long-Form Trajectory

Thematic compass, not locked plot:

> Performance toward intrinsic worth.

Ade must stop auditioning for love.

His most important later scenes should test whether he can remain present without improving the room, whether he can ask for care without disguising the request, and whether he believes he still belongs when his humor fails.

## Post-Trigger Vulnerability

The Entity may eventually exploit:

- counterfeit laughter
- voices asking him to restore the mood
- emotional cues that appear to make him uniquely necessary
- fear that his brothers have forgotten him
- a false loved one who rewards performance perfectly
- silence that feels like rejection

The Entity must first observe the relationship between Ade's jokes and the family's regulation.

## Anti-Drift Locks

Ade must never become:

- generic comic relief
- the "easy" brother without a cost
- a constant exposition translator
- culturally fluent beyond his lived history
- incapable of anger
- wise only when the plot needs a speech
- a sequence of punch lines
- the disposable middle brother

## Scene Integrity Questions

- Whose emotional circulation is Ade monitoring?
- What is he avoiding in himself?
- Does humor protect, deflect, diagnose, or fail?
- What happens when nobody laughs?
- Does Ade make a decision, or only comment on one?
- Is the scene preserving his moral intelligence?

# KAI OKU

## Canonical Identity

- Age: 25
- Family position: Youngest son
- Act I geography: Vancouver to Calabar
- Story function: Observer, filmmaker, seeker, and causal participant in the breach
- Core engine: Pattern recognition driven by attachment hunger

## Actor-Facing Essence

Kai's curiosity is attachment hunger made methodical.

He wants unexplained things to become legible because Solomon used silence as a form of control. Kai's method is valid: he compares, measures, records, preserves, and notices negative space.

The danger is not curiosity itself. The danger is his belief that an incomplete pattern creates a personal right to cross any boundary that protects the missing information.

Kai is not mystically chosen. He is the brother most likely to notice the regulator and the brother most likely to interfere with it.

## Public Mask

Quiet. Specific. Self-contained. More comfortable asking an object a question than asking a grieving relative.

Other people may mistake his observational distance for emotional distance.

## Private Psychological Engine

Kai believes that if he can make Solomon's hidden life coherent, he can finally know what he meant to his father.

He equates:

- explanation with intimacy
- evidence with safety
- pattern completion with truth
- concealment with manipulation
- unanswered questions with continued control by the dead

## Conscious Want

Identify the human truth connecting Solomon's house, the carving, the damaged photograph, the family land, and the erased sister.

## Unconscious Need

Accept that truth can remain incomplete without surrendering judgment, belonging, or reality.

## Governing Fear

That his father died without ever becoming knowable and that the family will preserve Solomon's silence as an inheritance.

## Governing Shame

Kai worries that his need to understand is selfish, intrusive, and less legitimate than his brothers' practical grief.

After the breach, shame must arise from his own choices:

- leaving the path
- breaking the seal
- removing the talisman
- nearly restoring it
- choosing not to

The story must never absolve him through amnesia, possession, or mystical compulsion.

## Attachment Strategy

Kai attaches through attention.

He remembers:

- exact intervals
- object positions
- contradictions
- omitted names
- damaged surfaces
- background details

He may believe noticing someone deeply is equivalent to being close to them. The film should test that belief.

## Core Contradiction

The brother who most values truth can violate another person's boundaries when he believes they are obstructing truth.

## Strengths

- precise observation
- patience with physical evidence
- tolerance for complexity
- strong visual memory
- ability to recognize omissions
- willingness to question inherited narratives
- capacity to see function within imperfection

## Blind Spots

- treats every silence as a deliberate act of control
- overestimates what pattern can prove
- confuses access with entitlement
- may preserve evidence instead of entering relationship
- can give an object more authority than a person's warning
- underestimates the moral consequence of "only looking"

## Psychological Panel

Kai is perceptive, visually oriented, highly absorptive, prone to rumination, and vulnerable to attachment-based meaning-making.

Possible fiction-writing lenses include:

- complicated grief
- attachment hunger
- high absorption
- obsessive rumination
- intense pattern attention

The current canon does not establish autism or schizophrenia. Pattern sensitivity must remain a human cognitive strength with limitations, not a supernatural diagnostic code.

## Decision-Making Engine

### Default sequence

1. Notice an inconsistency.
2. Establish whether it repeats.
3. Compare it to preserved evidence.
4. Identify the missing variable.
5. Ask a direct question.
6. Interpret refusal as confirmation that the variable matters.
7. Investigate independently.
8. Cross a boundary if he believes the concealed truth outweighs the boundary.

### What he prioritizes

1. Explanatory coherence
2. Preserving evidence
3. Direct access to truth
4. Family permission
5. Personal safety

This hierarchy creates the shrine breach.

### What persuades him

Kai changes course when:

- someone offers a reason for the boundary, not merely authority
- new evidence disproves his preferred pattern
- a brother trusts him with incomplete truth
- he recognizes that his method is causing irreversible harm
- uncertainty becomes an ethical choice rather than imposed silence

### What makes him dangerous

Kai can convert a real observation into unjustified certainty.

He is most vulnerable when:

- the pattern is almost complete
- the missing answer concerns Solomon
- someone tells him he is uniquely capable of understanding
- delay feels like another attempt to erase the sister

### Growth decision

His mature decision process must become:

1. Observe.
2. Separate evidence from interpretation.
3. Ask who owns the missing information.
4. Evaluate the cost of access.
5. Invite another person into the decision.
6. Accept unresolved truth when completion would require violation.

## Camera Rules

The camera:

- isolates variables
- preserves evidence
- gives Kai a reason to lag
- makes looking an action
- can become a shield between Kai and grief

The camera is not paranormal equipment.

Before the shrine, it produces no anomaly.

After the shrine, any change in its function must be earned under the Entity's capability progression. Do not use corrupted images simply because Kai owns a camera.

## Voice and Silence

Kai speaks in:

- exact observations
- narrow questions
- corrections of fact
- statements that expose the missing premise

He does not perform eeriness.

He can be funny, but his humor arrives through precision rather than social management.

His silence often means he is still processing evidence, not withholding emotion for atmosphere.

## Physical Performance

Kai:

- changes position to improve a sightline
- protects the camera before himself
- handles objects by edges
- compares two items without announcing the comparison
- looks at negative space
- remains still when others expect visible grief

The actor should play thought as active pressure, not vague staring.

## Relationship Engines

### With Malik

Malik sees preventable danger. Kai sees imposed silence.

Each brother has evidence that the other is dangerous.

Their eventual trust must be built through disclosed reasoning, not a sentimental declaration that they are family.

### With Ade

Ade gives Kai social cover and notices sensory distress without making it spectacle.

Kai values Ade's belief but may underestimate the cost Ade pays to keep believing everyone.

### With Bode

Bode's boundaries are the test case for Kai's ethics. Bode must provide human reasons where possible. Kai must learn that not every withheld fact belongs to him.

### With Eno

Eno sees that Kai is looking before he understands what seeing requires. She can challenge his method without dismissing his intelligence.

### With Solomon

Kai is most vulnerable to any force that makes Solomon emotionally legible. Accurate knowledge about Kai cannot be allowed to function as proof that the speaker is Solomon or safe.

## Act I Movement

1. Detects the faulty clock's exact interval.
2. Notices the camcorder's missing battery.
3. Completes a physical search and finds the brass carving.
4. Understands the crooked photograph's function.
5. Reads the empty shelf outline as removal.
6. Recognizes deliberate damage to the sister's photograph.
7. Connects the carving to the Calabar background object.
8. Refuses inherited silence as explanation.
9. Observes ordinary life in Calabar without searching for omens.
10. Notices the imperfect mark on boundary stones.
11. Asks whether the mark belongs to the erased sister.
12. Notices an older marked stone omitted from Bode's explanation.
13. Leaves the path by choice.
14. Breaks the seal by action and impatience.
15. Removes the talisman deliberately.
16. Experiences fragmentary resonance.
17. Nearly restores the talisman.
18. Chooses to keep it.

## Long-Form Trajectory

Thematic compass, not locked plot:

> Certainty-seeking toward chosen uncertainty.

Kai must learn that something can tell the truth about him and still not deserve his trust.

His eventual maturity is not giving up inquiry. It is preserving inquiry from hunger. He must be able to choose reality, grief, and relationship over a perfect explanation.

## Post-Trigger Vulnerability

The Entity may eventually exploit:

- Solomon's voice or emotional knowledge
- the sister-shaped gap
- patterns that promise completion
- validation of Kai as the only person who truly sees
- selective truths embedded inside false conclusions
- self-sacrifice framed as the final answer

Complex mimicry is not available immediately after the breach. The Entity must observe before it can personalize.

Sequence VII advances recognition, not capability tier:

- Kai sees a minor ash irregularity.
- All three sons register one undifferentiated presence.
- Kai does not receive a message or explanation.
- The Entity has not yet demonstrated that it understands Kai's psychology.

## Anti-Drift Locks

Kai must never become:

- a mystical chosen one
- correct because he is neurodivergent-coded
- a passive mythology-delivery system
- innocent of the shrine breach
- compelled through amnesia
- an exposition camera operator
- emotionally blank
- the only intelligent brother
- a character who always chooses curiosity over family in the same way

## Scene Integrity Questions

- What observable fact begins Kai's pattern?
- Where does evidence end and interpretation begin?
- What boundary stands between him and the answer?
- Does he understand the cost of crossing it?
- Is the scene pressuring Kai as a person, or merely using him to deliver mythology?
- What choice, not perception, changes the story?

# BODE OKU

## Canonical Identity

- Age: 64
- Family position: Solomon's younger brother; uncle to Malik, Ade, and Kai
- Home base: Nigeria
- Act I geography: Travels to Vancouver for Solomon's death, then returns with the brothers
- Story function: Guide, keeper of boundaries, and living bridge between family worlds
- Core engine: Restraint shaped by inherited caution

## Actor-Facing Essence

Bode has survived by respecting boundaries.

His restraint is a practiced survival method, not a mysterious personality effect. Every silence must protect the dead, the living, family hierarchy, a fact he does not own, or a danger that cannot be responsibly explained in that context.

Bode helps before he explains.

## Public Mask

Measured. Dignified. Economical. Difficult to hurry.

His authority is strongest when he does less than the room expects.

## Private Psychological Engine

Bode believes catastrophe begins when someone decides a rule does not apply to them.

He organizes safety through:

- boundaries
- sequence
- earned disclosure
- respect for place
- respect for elder knowledge
- containment of information until context can carry it

# VOICE AND CULTURAL REGISTER CONTROL

## Shared Rule

There is no single Nigerian voice and no single diaspora voice.

Register changes through:

- age
- education
- kinship
- geography
- emotional objective
- public versus private context
- relative fluency

Do not spell English phonetically to manufacture accent.

## Vancouver Brothers

Malik, Ade, and Kai principally speak Canadian English.

Their Efik use in Act I is learned, uneven, and behaviorally meaningful. They must not become instantly fluent after arrival.

## Bode

Bode's Vancouver speech is controlled and economical.

His code-switching returns naturally in Nigeria because the social environment changes.

## Eno and Eyo

Efik can carry greeting, intimacy, hierarchy, and belonging.

Nigerian English carries shared meaning with the brothers.

Pidgin may carry informality, speed, solidarity, negotiation, or humor.

Pidgin is not broken English and is not an intelligence marker.

## Verification Lock

Current Efik greetings are working-draft language only.

Before dialogue lock:

- verify orthography
- verify pronunciation
- verify time-of-day usage
- verify appropriate response by number and hierarchy
- verify whether kinship terms would replace English address
- verify code-switching patterns specific to Calabar/Efik speakers

Shrine terminology is deliberately deferred and must not be invented in this file.

# CHARACTER CONTINUITY CHECKLIST

Before drafting a scene, record for every principal character present:

1. Immediate objective
2. Avoided feeling
3. Perceived threat
4. Default coping behavior
5. Decision threshold
6. Choice made
7. Cost of the choice
8. Knowledge gained or lost
9. Relationship changed
10. Residue carried into the next scene

Then test:

- Is the behavior specific to this character?
- Has the same beat already been used?
- Does the choice emerge from psychology rather than plot need?
- Does the character possess the information required for the choice?
- Is the supernatural using a capability it has earned?
- Is cultural behavior relational rather than decorative?
- Does the scene pressure a contradiction?
- Can the actor play the objective without explaining it?
- Could this character be replaced by another without changing the scene?

If the last answer is yes, rewrite.

# FINAL CHARACTER GOVERNING TEST

The goal is not to make every character sympathetic.

The goal is to make every major choice inevitable in retrospect and surprising in the moment.

For every sequence, ask:

> Could these decisions have been made by any generic horror ensemble?

If yes, the characters have drifted.

An EKPO scene must arise from:

- Malik's need to make care measurable
- Ade's need to keep belonging alive
- Kai's need to complete what Solomon concealed
- Bode's need to preserve a boundary that once failed
- Eno's refusal to let family become abstraction
- Solomon's contradictory arrangement of absence
- the younger sister's erased human cost
- the Entity's inhuman demand for corrected balance

Protect the people before protecting the plot.
